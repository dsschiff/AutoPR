🚨 Automation is reshaping the future of work—and Norbert Wiener saw it coming decades ago. Our new analysis examines what we can learn from the "father of cybernetics" to ensure technology serves humanity. Published in IEEE Transactions on Technology and Society: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10798994

⚡ The Challenge: Automation threatens millions of jobs, but the deeper issue is preserving human dignity and meaningful work. Wiener warned of a "factory without employees"—a world where humans compete with their own creations.

💡 Wiener's Core Insight: Technology isn't neutral. In 1949, he told union leader Walter Reuther: "I do not wish to contribute to selling labor down the river." Technological progress must go hand-in-hand with ethical foresight.

🔍 Key findings from our IEEE workshop analysis:
• Automation risks eroding meaningful work—the creativity, purpose, and social connections that make labor fulfilling
• Benefits often concentrate among few, worsening inequality  
• Regulation lags behind innovation, creating governance gaps

🎯 What's at stake: Work provides identity, community, and purpose—not just paychecks. We need policies that center human values, not just economic efficiency.

🌟 The opportunity: With right frameworks, automation can expand access to education and healthcare, free humans for creative work, and address systemic inequalities. But this requires rethinking success metrics—from profit to human flourishing.

How can we ensure automation enhances rather than erodes human dignity and purpose? The question isn't whether automation will reshape work—it's whether we'll shape automation to serve humanity.
