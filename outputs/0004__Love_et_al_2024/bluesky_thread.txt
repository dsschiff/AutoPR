🚨 Automation is reshaping work—and Norbert Wiener saw it coming decades ago.

What can we learn from the "father of cybernetics" to ensure technology serves humanity?

Our new analysis of Wiener's legacy 👇

https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10798994

---

⚡ The Problem: Automation's Double-Edged Sword

From factory floors to offices, automation threatens millions of jobs. But it's deeper than efficiency—it's about human dignity and the meaning of work.

Wiener warned of a "factory without employees."

---

💡 Wiener's Core Insight: Tech Isn't Neutral

In 1949, he told union leader Walter Reuther: "I do not wish to contribute to selling labor down the river."

Technological progress must go hand-in-hand with ethical foresight.

---

🔍 Key Findings from IEEE Workshop Analysis:

• Automation risks eroding meaningful work—creativity, purpose, social connections
• Benefits concentrate among few, worsening inequality
• Regulation lags behind innovation, creating governance gaps

---

🎯 What's at Stake: Work as Human Identity

Work isn't just a paycheck—it's identity, community, purpose. Automation risks reducing it to cold metrics.

We need policies that center human values, not just economic efficiency.

---

📚 Historical Echo: The Real Luddites

Think Luddites were anti-tech? Wrong. They fought a system using technology to exploit workers.

Same patterns today: displacement, inequality, "progress for the few."

---

🌟 The Opportunity: Automation for Good

With right frameworks, automation can:
• Expand access to education/healthcare
• Free humans for creative work
• Address inequalities

Requires rethinking success—from profit to human flourishing.

---

🔮 Wiener's Wisdom: Embrace Uncertainty

He knew we can't predict automation's full effects. The takeaway? Stay adaptable, proactive, humble.

Ethical foresight isn't one-time—it's ongoing.
