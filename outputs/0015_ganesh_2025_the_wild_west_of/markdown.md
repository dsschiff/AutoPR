🔥 "Wild West" AI in Radiology? Here's how clinicians are stepping up where governance lags. A gripping saga of responsibility, tech, & resilience 🧵:  

---

1/ The big reveal: AI adoption in hospitals is booming 🚀, with dozens of FDA-approved AI tools deployed in radiology 🩻. From detecting abnormalities to triaging cases, it’s transforming the field. But here’s the problem: governance hasn’t kept up. And that’s *dangerous*.  

2/ Imagine this: a multi-million-dollar AI tool misclassifies a tumor. Who’s responsible? The radiologist? The AI vendor? The hospital? This "Wild West" scenario of unclear roles & siloed protocols fuels chaos.  

Our new paper dives into how clinicians are filling gaps in AI governance & why it matters.  

---

3/ Radiology is AI’s prime testing ground—so what’s the deal? Our qualitative study interviewed 22 radiologists, clinicians, & regulators in the 🇺🇸 US & 🇬🇧 UK. What we found is both inspiring AND concerning:  

🚩 *No uniform AI governance practice.*  
🚩 *Inequities in resources & expertise.*  
🚩 *Powerful clinician-led solutions.*  

Let’s unpack the story:  

---

4/ **Key insight #1: A “Wild West” of governance** 🤠  
Some hospitals have robust AI committees for oversight. Others just wing it—relying on clinicians to manage risks without tools, guidance, or institutional support.  

Figure: ![Figure 0](./img/img_0.jpg)  

Even centralized initiatives, like England's AI Diagnostic Fund, fail to address *local complexities*. The result? Fragmented safety standards that *jeopardize patient care*.  

---

5/ **Key insight #2: The “Moral Crumple Zone”**   
Clinicians face impossible dilemmas. AI errors often leave radiologists holding the bag—they’re legally liable for decisions driven by machine outcomes they didn’t *fully* control. 😨  

And yet...  

🛑 Most hospitals lack requirements for post-deployment AI monitoring.  
🛑 Who checks if models fail on diverse local data? Often, NO ONE.  

This "accountability gap" is a ticking time bomb.  

---

6/ **Key insight #3: The rise of AI “Governance Entrepreneurs”** 🔎  

When institutions fail, clinicians step up. We call them “governance entrepreneurs”—radiologists, IT staff, ethicists, & others who fill this void voluntarily. They:  

💡 Vet AI vendors.  
💡 Create makeshift performance audits.  
💡 Push hospitals to monitor for bias + safety.  

But here’s the kicker: THEY LACK RESOURCES. Most of them work off-hours, unpaid, navigating bureaucracies that don’t officially recognize their roles.  

Figure: ![Figure 1](./img/img_1.jpg)  

---

7/ So why the chaos? It's a classic *top-down vs. bottom-up* conflict.  

Centralized “top-down” AI regulations fail to address local-level nuances:  
📊 AI tools don’t perform equally across patient populations.  
💻 Variations in hospital data pipelines create edge cases.  

What healthcare needs is a *hybrid model* that balances global oversight with local customization.  

---

8/ **The systemic barriers are real** 🛑  

💸 *Funding inequity* exists. Small hospitals can’t match the resources of large teaching hospitals.  

⏰ Procurement hurdles + limited access to local datasets make it near impossible to test AI before deployment (*especially in NHS trusts*).  

🎓 Clinicians often lack formal training in AI literacy—too many rely on vendor promises instead of proof.  

These issues worsen disparities in patient safety & equity.  

---

🛠️ Here's what needs to happen:  

1️⃣ **Streamline regulatory guidance.** Simplify fuzzy rules from agencies like FDA/MHRA. Hospitals need clarity.  

2️⃣ **Mandatory AI education.** Teach radiologists to audit AI bias, spot performance drifts, & resist vendor hype.  

3️⃣ **Data transparency.** Imagine a trusted “Consumer Reports” for medical AI. Comparing tools pre-adoption could save lives.  

4️⃣ **Local & global synergy.** Hybrid frameworks must empower grassroots governance entrepreneurs AND enforce standardized monitoring.  

5️⃣ **Fund the under-resourced.** We must invest in hospitals with fewer means to ensure equity in adoption.  

---

💬 Final thought: AI promises to *revolutionize* healthcare—but without equitable and robust governance, we risk a future of inconsistency, liability, and lower trust in models.  

Hospitals, regulators, & clinicians: let's learn from radiology's grassroots trailblazers. 🌱
