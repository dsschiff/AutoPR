🚨 The AI job market is evolving—and it's not just about coding anymore. Employers are demanding skills in **AI ethics** and **governance** at an unprecedented rate. Here's what 4M+ job postings from 2018-2023 reveal about the future of AI careers. 🧵👇

---

1/ **The Problem:**  
AI is transforming industries, but with great power comes great responsibility. Enter the "AI skills gap"—a shortage of professionals equipped with not only technical expertise but also the ability to handle **ethical dilemmas** and **regulatory challenges**.  

---

2/ **The Study:**  
Researchers analyzed **4.4M+ AI-related job postings** to uncover trends in demand for **AI ethics** (fairness, transparency) and **AI governance** (regulatory compliance, risk management). Here's what they found:  

**![Figure 0](./img/img_0.jpg)**  

---

3/ **Key Insight 1: Core Skills**  
AI ethics ≠ AI governance. Employers seek distinct but complementary skills:  
- **Ethics roles:** Data privacy, bias mitigation, critical thinking.  
- **Governance roles:** Risk management, policy development, leadership.  
Both require *interdisciplinary knowledge*.  

---

4/ **Key Insight 2: Rising Demand**  
Demand for ethics and governance professionals is surging 🚀:  
- AI ethics roles grew from **35K in 2018** to **109K in 2022**.  
- Governance roles followed a similar trajectory, hitting **96K in 2022**.  
Even as overall AI hiring dipped in 2023, these roles remained stable.  

**![Figure 1](./img/img_1.jpg)**  

---

5/ **Key Insight 3: Industry Leaders**  
Not all sectors are moving at the same pace. The **Finance** and **Information** industries dominate demand, with AI ethics/governance roles growing fastest there. Why? Highly regulated sectors can't afford ethical lapses.  

**![Figure 2](./img/img_2.jpg)**  

---

6/ **What’s Driving This?**  
Stakeholders are aligning:  
- **Governments**: New AI regulations demand compliance.  
- **Employers**: Public trust is critical for AI adoption.  
- **Education**: Universities are racing to create relevant programs.  

**![Figure 3](./img/img_3.jpg)**  

---

7/ **The Opportunity:**  
More than 100K professionals are needed annually in these fields. Yet, many organizations struggle to find talent. For jobseekers, this is a golden moment to upskill in **AI ethics** and **governance**—fields where demand outpaces supply.  

---

8/ **The Big Picture:**  
AI ethics and governance aren't just "nice-to-haves." They're becoming **non-negotiable** pillars of responsible AI development. As industries adopt AI at scale, these roles will define how society benefits—or suffers—from this powerful technology.  

---

Should every AI professional be trained in ethics and governance, or should these remain specialized roles? Let’s discuss how to build a workforce equipped for the AI age.
