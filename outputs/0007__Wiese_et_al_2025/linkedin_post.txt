The AI job market is evolving beyond coding. Our analysis of 4.4M+ job postings from 2018-2023 reveals employers now demand AI ethics and governance skills at unprecedented rates.

https://doi.org/10.1109/TTS.2025.3567143

✅ Key findings from our study published in IEEE Transactions on Technology and Society:

• AI ethics roles grew from 35K in 2018 to 109K in 2022
• Governance roles hit 96K in 2022, following similar trajectory
• Even as overall AI hiring dipped in 2023, these roles remained stable

➤ Ethics vs. Governance: Distinct but complementary skill sets
Ethics roles focus on data privacy, bias mitigation, and critical thinking. Governance roles emphasize risk management, policy development, and leadership. Both require interdisciplinary knowledge.

➤ Industry leaders: Finance and Information sectors dominate demand
Highly regulated industries are driving growth fastest—they can't afford ethical lapses as AI adoption scales.

• More than 100K professionals needed annually
• Many organizations struggle to find qualified talent
• Significant opportunity for those entering the field

Results suggest AI ethics and governance are becoming non-negotiable pillars of responsible AI development. As industries adopt AI at scale, these roles will define how society benefits from this powerful technology.
