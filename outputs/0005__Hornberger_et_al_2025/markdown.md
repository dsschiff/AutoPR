1/ 🚨 AI is taking over industries, but are students prepared? 🤔 A groundbreaking study across Germany, the UK, and the US reveals shocking gaps in AI literacy among future professionals. Here's what we found—and why it matters: 🧵

2/ 🌍 This is the first large-scale multinational study of AI literacy among university students. 1,465 students were assessed on AI knowledge, self-confidence (self-efficacy), interest, attitudes, and prior experiences. The results? Eye-opening.  

3/ 📊 Germany leads in *AI literacy*—outscoring the UK & US. Why? Better integration of AI coursework in education. But the US has the highest *self-efficacy* (confidence in AI skills). The UK? High skepticism, with the most *negative attitudes* toward AI.

![Figure 0](./img/img_0.jpg)

4/ 🤔 But here’s the kicker: Confidence doesn’t equal competence. High self-efficacy in the US often didn’t align with actual AI knowledge. Overconfidence could lead to poor decision-making in tech-driven careers. Germany's model? Competence *and* caution.

5/ 🧠 AI literacy isn’t just about coding. It’s understanding AI’s ethics, strengths, weaknesses, & societal impacts. Yet, **over 70% of students across all countries haven’t taken an AI-related course.** Germany's 26.5% participation leads, but it’s far from ideal.

![Figure 1](./img/img_1.jpg)

6/ 📚 Where are students learning about AI? Informally. Tools like ChatGPT spark interest, but few dive deeper. In Germany, 30% say they *never* explore AI outside class. In the US, only 3% engage daily. The appetite for AI knowledge exists—education isn’t keeping up.

![Figure 2](./img/img_2.jpg)

7/ 🔧 So, what’s missing in education? Ethics is the least-covered topic in AI courses, despite its critical importance. Germany, surprisingly, lags here. Only 6.6% of students say ethics is addressed “a lot.” Yet ethical AI is at the heart of public trust.

![Figure 3](./img/img_3.jpg)

8/ 💡 The study also validated a robust AI literacy test for global use. This tool could reshape how we assess, benchmark, and improve AI education worldwide. But the findings underscore a need for tailored approaches—one size doesn’t fit all.

![Figure 4](./img/img_4.jpg)

9/ 🚀 Recommendations for action:
   - Educators: Bridge gaps between confidence & competence with hands-on, interdisciplinary AI learning.
   - Policymakers: Fund AI literacy programs & emphasize ethics.
   - Students: Engage with AI beyond “easy tools” like ChatGPT.

10/ 🌟 The takeaway? AI literacy is the *key competency* of the 21st century. Future leaders need more than interest or confidence—they need robust knowledge & critical thinking to navigate an AI-driven world. Let’s close the gap.
