1/ AI isn't just transforming technology—it's reshaping global power. But how do we govern a force this disruptive? 🌍🤖

Enter Daniel S. Schiff's work: a glimpse into the future of AI governance. Here's how aviation, climate policy, and open-source software might hold the key: 🧵

2/ The challenge: AI regulation is pulling us in 5 directions at once:  
- Centralized vs. decentralized control  
- Robust vs. minimal enforcement  
- Adaptive vs. enduring rules  
- Private leadership vs. shared governance  
- Narrow vs. broad societal goals  

Balancing these is no easy task. ![Figure 0](./img/img_0.jpg)

3/ To navigate this chaos, Schiff's paper adapts lessons from 4 existing governance models:  
✈️ Aviation: Centralized, safety-first, but costly  
🌱 Organic food: Decentralized, private-driven, inconsistent  
🌍 Climate: Shared, flexible, but weak enforcement  
💻 Open-source: Collaborative, adaptive, but fragile  

![Figure 1](./img/img_1.jpg)

4/ Here's where we stand today:  
- The EU is centralizing with the AI Act. The US? Decentralized and sector-specific.  
- Monitoring is a patchwork: from strict compliance to voluntary audits.  
- Tech firms dominate decisions, while public input is often sidelined.  

Fragmentation is the norm.

5/ But the real wildcard? The pace of AI itself.  
Generative AI exploded onto the scene, leaving policymakers scrambling.  
A major crisis—like a deepfake election scandal—could force rapid regulation.  
Meanwhile, public demand for accountability grows louder every day.

6/ So, where do we go from here?  
Schiff calls for adaptive, hybrid models that combine:  
- Centralized safety oversight (like aviation)  
- Decentralized innovation hubs (like open-source software)  
- Broad societal goals (like climate policy)  

Experimentation will be key.

7/ Why does this matter? AI governance will shape not just tech, but society itself.  
Done right, it could address inequality, climate impact, and more.  
Done wrong, it risks entrenching power imbalances and exacerbating harms.  

The stakes couldn’t be higher.

8/ What trade-offs would *you* prioritize in AI governance? Centralization or flexibility? Private leadership or public control?  

Let’s discuss. The future of AI isn’t just a tech issue—it’s a human one. 🌍🤖
