How did AI policymaking go from bipartisan collaboration to partisan gridlock in just a few years? 🧵

Our new study tracks this shift across 44 states (2019-2022) and reveals the triggers that fractured early cooperation.

https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/poi3.70020

---

🎯 The setup: AI policy began as a rare blank slate—no entrenched partisan playbooks.

Legislators tackled transparency, privacy, and ethics collaboratively. Over 150 AI-related bills emerged across states during this window.

---

💡 Early wins focused on "soft governance"—transparency requirements, research initiatives—to avoid polarizing debates.

• Illinois (2019): Hiring algorithm disclosure
• Idaho: Pretrial risk assessments framed as governance reform

---

🤔 But by 2022, bipartisan windows narrowed.

We identify 4 key triggers of polarization:
1️⃣ Competing problem definitions
2️⃣ Divergent policy tools
3️⃣ Stakeholder dynamics
4️⃣ Strategic "subsystem shopping"

---

🔑 Trigger 1—Problem definitions:

Early AI framing was broad: transparency, privacy, ethics. But when linked to racial equity or redistribution, partisan divides flared.

• Dems: Equity-focused reforms
• GOP: Industry self-regulation

[Attach image: img_1.jpg]

---

🔧 Trigger 2—Policy tools:

Consensus around "soft" tools (notifications, research) gave way to conflict over "hard" tools (demographic audits, stricter regulation).

Republicans favored innovation-friendly approaches; Democrats pushed robust oversight.

---

🤝 Trigger 3—Stakeholders:

Academic experts initially fostered neutrality. But as private-sector lobbying ramped up, partisan dynamics intensified.

Example: Bail insurers in Idaho backed bipartisan regulation early on.

---

🎯 "Subsystem shopping":

Policy entrepreneurs strategically framed AI to sidestep polarization.

Colorado combined education & law enforcement frameworks for facial recognition to avoid criminal justice conflicts.

---

🚨 By 2022, partisan cleavages hardened:

• Republicans: Pro-AI in defense, law enforcement
• Democrats: Focused on risks, equity concerns

This mirrored broader ideological divides over regulation and government intervention.

---

📚 Published in Policy & Internet. Results suggest policymakers can sustain collaboration by framing AI around transparency & innovation, starting with soft governance, and leveraging nonpartisan experts.
