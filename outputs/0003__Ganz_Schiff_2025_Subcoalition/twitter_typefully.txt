Organizations aren't unified entities—they're arenas of competing stakeholder goals and hidden factions.

Our new paper in Management Science introduces Subcoalition Cluster Analysis (SCA) to uncover invisible coalitions driving conflict 🧵

https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/

---

Most management models treat organizations as monolithic. Reality? Teams form subcoalitions with aligned preferences.

These fault lines drive decision-making chaos—but they're hard to observe and even harder to model with traditional methods.

---

SCA works like a smarter, preference-based clustering algorithm. It groups organizational actors into subcoalitions by maximizing shared utility.

Result? A clear map of internal divisions, even with sparse or messy data 📊

[Attach image: img_0.jpg]

---

Unlike traditional clustering methods that assume external factors drive preferences, SCA flips the script:

✅ Coalitions form organically from shared goals
✅ Bottom-up, relational approach
✅ Built for messy real-world data

---

Case study: Wikipedia's 2013 Visual Editor rollout sparked rebellion from veteran editors.

SCA revealed two warring subcoalitions:
🛠️ Technical design focus
🌍 Cultural norms focus

Leadership missed these divisions—leading to strategy disaster

---

Baseball Hall of Fame case (2009-2016): Writers debated inducting players linked to PEDs.

SCA uncovered a growing rift:
⚾ Pro-PED forgiveness coalition
⚾ Anti-PED purist coalition

This schism reinforced decision-making silos over time

✅ Identify & anticipate internal conflicts

💻 Handles incomplete data across industries
