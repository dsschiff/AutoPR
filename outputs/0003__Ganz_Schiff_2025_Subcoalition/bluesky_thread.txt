Organizations aren't unified entities—they're arenas of competing goals and hidden factions.

Our new paper in Management Science introduces Subcoalition Cluster Analysis (SCA), a method to uncover invisible coalitions driving conflict 🧵

https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/mnsc.2020.0001

---

Most models treat organizations as monolithic. Reality? Teams form subcoalitions with aligned preferences.

These fault lines drive decision chaos—but they're hard to observe and model with traditional methods.

---

SCA works like smarter preference-based clustering. Groups actors by maximizing shared utility.

Result? Clear maps of internal divisions, even with sparse data 📊

---

Unlike traditional clustering, SCA shows coalitions form organically from shared goals:

✅ Bottom-up, relational approach
✅ Built for messy real-world data
✅ Reveals hidden organizational fault lines
