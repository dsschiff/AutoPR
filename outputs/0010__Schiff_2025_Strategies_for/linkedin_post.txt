🚨 AI governance is in crisis. Over 500 fragmented standards, overlapping frameworks, and contradictory regulations create a compliance maze for organizations. Published in the Handbook of Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence, our analysis reveals a path forward. https://link.springer.com/rwe/10.1007/978-981-97-8440-0_82-1

🤔 The core challenges:

➤ **Translation gaps**: Converting ethical concepts like fairness and transparency into actionable standards risks oversimplification or "ethics-washing"

➤ **Voluntary compliance**: Without enforcement, organizations cherry-pick standards, creating superficial audits instead of accountability

➤ **Decision paralysis**: The "15 competing standards" problem leaves organizations without clear guidance

➤ **Pacing mismatch**: Standards take years to develop while AI risks emerge in months

➤ **Geopolitical fragmentation**: Divergent approaches across EU, US, China make global alignment nearly impossible

🚀 **Our roadmap for human-centered AI governance:**

✅ **Harmonize globally**: Collaborate across ISO, IEEE, OECD using crosswalks to reduce redundancy

✅ **Layer strategically**: High-level frameworks → domain guidelines → operational tools

✅ **Build agility**: Living documents, rapid-response taskforces, regulatory sandboxes

✅ **Strengthen auditing**: Independent accreditation, transparency, practical implementation tools

✅ **Center stakeholders**: Include marginalized communities, Global South voices, citizen assemblies

The stakes are high: harmonized governance could transform AI into a trustworthy societal tool, while fragmentation risks ethics-washing and widening inequities. For policy practitioners, governance teams, and org leaders - the time to act is now.
