1/ 🚨 AI isn’t just reshaping work—it’s *extracting* it. From copyrighted books to professional norms, AI’s rise is built on systems of value extraction that mirror mining and energy industries. What does this mean for labor, power, and equity? Let’s dive in: 🧵  

2/ 🤔 **The Big Question:**  
As AI automates and commodifies, who benefits—and who is left behind? A groundbreaking study from James, Schiff, Love, et al. (2025) asks: how does AI extract value from work, destabilize professions, and deepen systemic inequalities?  

3/ 💡 **Key Insight:**  
AI isn’t just a tool; it’s an *extractive system*. Think of it as a digital mining operation—appropriating copyrighted material (books, code, art) to fuel innovation while bypassing consent, compensation, or accountability.  

4/ 📚 For example, millions of books were destroyed to train Large Language Models (LLMs). The cost? Not just intellectual property, but cultural heritage. AI’s innovation often comes at the expense of creators and communities.  

5/ 🏢 **Power Dynamics:**  
AI centralizes power in tech monopolies and wealthy nations. Smaller firms and countries become dependent, while tech giants influence policies to cement their dominance—a classic feedback loop of inequality.  

6/ 📉 **The Social Fallout:**  
AI adoption isn’t always ethical or careful. In schools and workplaces, rushed implementations erode intellectual rigor and professional autonomy. Overreliance on AI tools is replacing critical thinking with shortcuts.  

7/ 🙋‍♀️ **Feminist & Social Lens:**  
The study coins terms like "machine learning misogyny" to highlight how AI amplifies societal biases, from gender discrimination to harassment. Tech isn’t neutral—it reflects (and often worsens) the inequalities it’s trained on.  

8/ 🤖 **What Needs to Change:**  
The authors argue it’s time to move from efficiency-at-all-costs to a governance model that prioritizes equity, sustainability, and human dignity. AI isn’t just a tech problem—it’s a societal one, demanding multidisciplinary solutions.  

9/ 🌍 **A Call to Action:**  
To ensure AI serves everyone, not just the powerful, we need transparency, diverse perspectives, and policies that hold tech accountable. This isn’t just about innovation—it’s about justice.
