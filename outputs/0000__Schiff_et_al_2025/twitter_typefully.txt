AI systems decide hiring, loans, healthcare. But who's auditing the auditors? 🤖

Our new study in Big Data & Society explores AI ethics auditing—who does it, how it works, and what's missing.

https://doi.org/10.1177/20539517241299732 🧵

---

We interviewed 34 AI ethics auditors across 23 organizations in 7 countries to understand this rapidly evolving field.

Think financial audits, but for AI: planning scope, validating models, analyzing risks, and reporting findings. 📊

[Attach image: img_0.jpg]

---

What's driving the AI auditing boom?

🔹 Regulation (EU AI Act, NIST frameworks)
🔹 Reputation risk (avoiding biased AI headlines)
🔹 Competitive advantage (trustworthy AI as strategy)

The ecosystem includes internal teams, Big Four firms, and specialized startups.

[Attach image: img_1.jpg]

---

Key finding: Most audits focus narrowly on technical metrics like bias and accuracy.

Broader socio-technical issues—how AI impacts vulnerable communities—often get sidelined.

And external reporting? Almost nonexistent. Transparency remains a major gap. 📈

[Attach image: img_2.jpg]

---

The challenges auditors face:

• Regulatory ambiguity (laws are vague or evolving)
• Data gaps (poor documentation/governance)
• Interdisciplinary friction (tech, legal, leadership misalignment)

Yet auditors are building the ecosystem from scratch.

[Attach image: img_3.jpg]

---

Despite hurdles, AI ethics auditors are ecosystem builders:

✅ Translating vague regulations into actionable frameworks
✅ Pushing organizations to improve AI governance
✅ Shaping best practices for emerging field

But gaps remain in stakeholder engagement and standardization.

---

The stakes are high. AI influences who gets hired, who gets loans, who receives healthcare.

Without robust, transparent audits, we risk perpetuating biases and undermining trust in AI systems.

The path forward requires clearer guidelines and public accountability.
