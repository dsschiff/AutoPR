AI systems are making critical decisions about hiring, loans, and healthcare—but who's ensuring these systems are fair and accountable? Our new research in Big Data & Society examines the rapidly emerging field of AI ethics auditing. https://doi.org/10.1177/20539517241299732

🔍 We interviewed 34 AI ethics auditors across 23 organizations in 7 countries to understand how this field is developing. Think of AI ethics audits as borrowing from financial audits: planning scope, validating models, analyzing risks, and reporting findings.

➤ What's driving the boom? Regulatory pressure (EU AI Act, NIST frameworks), reputation risk management, and the competitive advantage of trustworthy AI.

➤ The ecosystem includes internal audit teams, Big Four consulting firms, and specialized AI ethics startups—each bringing different approaches and capabilities.

• Key finding: Most audits focus narrowly on technical metrics like bias detection and model accuracy, while broader socio-technical impacts on vulnerable communities often get overlooked.

• Major gap: External reporting is almost nonexistent, limiting transparency and public accountability.

✅ Despite challenges like regulatory ambiguity and data governance gaps, auditors are building the ecosystem from scratch—translating vague regulations into actionable frameworks and pushing organizations toward better AI governance.

For AI governance teams and policy practitioners: The stakes are high. Without robust, transparent auditing practices, we risk perpetuating systemic biases and undermining public trust in AI systems.
