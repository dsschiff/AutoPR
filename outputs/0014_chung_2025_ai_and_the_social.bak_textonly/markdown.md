1/ Algorithms are now decision-makers in welfare, policing, hiring, and public policy. But here’s the chilling question: Do these AI systems uphold the same principles of legitimacy that we demand from human governments? ❓

👉 Spoiler: Not quite. Let’s unpack why and what can be done.

2/ Theories of political legitimacy—Hobbes, Locke, Rousseau, Rawls, Nozick—were built to justify rule by people or states. But AI flips the script, introducing transparency gaps, algorithmic bias, and global reach, fundamentally *stretching* these models. 🤖🌍

🔍 The big challenge? AI destabilizes three key anchors of legitimacy:
- **Accountability:** Who owns algorithmic mistakes?  
- **Consent:** Can anyone meaningfully “opt out” of AI governance?  
- **Fairness & Trust:** How can we measure justice in opaque, data-driven systems?
