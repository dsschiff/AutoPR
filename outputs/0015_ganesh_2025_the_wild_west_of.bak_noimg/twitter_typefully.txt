🚨 AI is transforming radiology—but governance frameworks are stuck in the "Wild West." Our new study reveals how clinicians are stepping up as governance entrepreneurs to fill critical gaps. Published in AIES 2025 🧵

https://doi.org/10.1609/aies.v8i2.36608

---

We interviewed 22 experts—radiologists, oncologists, regulators—across the US and UK to map AI governance in radiology. The findings? Striking gaps between regulation and reality. 🏥

---

What's a "moral crumple zone"? When clinicians are unfairly held accountable for AI errors they can't control—like vendor algorithms misdiagnosing on clinical data. A consequence of underdeveloped governance. 😤

---

AI governance operates across layers—federal (FDA, MHRA), regional, local hospital policies. But there's vast disparity in implementation depending on resources, expertise, and population needs. 🌍

---

Some hospitals meticulously validate AI with local data. Others deploy off-the-shelf algorithms with no safeguards. This inconsistency puts patients at risk of AI "drift"—when systems degrade over time. 📉

---

Clinicians are forming grassroots committees to monitor AI performance and develop guidelines. But these efforts are usually underfunded, voluntary, and exist within rigid healthcare hierarchies. 💪

---

Key obstacles our participants identified:
1️⃣ Limited AI literacy among staff
2️⃣ Undefined evaluation standards
3️⃣ Contradictory guidelines from regulators

Clinicians want streamlined, coherent policies. 🚩

---

We propose a hybrid governance model:
✅ Standardized national guidelines
✅ Localized oversight for unique populations
✅ Funding for underserved hospitals

Equity in governance ensures all facilities can benefit safely. ⚖️

---

Training is critical. Many clinicians lack AI literacy, leading to blind trust in vendor claims. Education could empower staff to ask tough questions about AI tools they're expected to use. 📚

---

For policy practitioners and governance teams: What should fair AI governance prioritize? Standardization? Local flexibility? Performance monitoring?

We'd love to hear your thoughts 👇
