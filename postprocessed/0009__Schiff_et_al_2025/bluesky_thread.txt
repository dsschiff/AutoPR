Are we failing to teach social responsibility in STEM? 🧵

New 5-year study of 124 Georgia Tech students reveals a troubling pattern: undergrads leave college LESS connected to societal impact than when they started.

[LINK]

@purduepolsci.bsky.social @GRAILcenter.bsky.social

---

📊 The study tracked Professional Connectedness—students' sense of connection to societal impact

Result: scores dropped significantly from 5.65 to 5.43 by graduation (p < 0.001)

Students increasingly prioritized salary over societal impact as career motivators

[Attach image: img_1.jpg]

---

💡 Plot twist: students DID grow in social awareness—just not from STEM classes

Biggest drivers were informal activities:
• Peer discussions
• Family conversations
• Self-guided exploration

Formal STEM education barely moved the needle

---

❌ Students described STEM courses as:
• Too technical (little ethics integration)
• Disconnected from societal dimensions  
• Career-focused but not impact-focused

This matters: STEM professionals shape decisions affecting entire societies

[Attach image: img_3.jpg]

---

🔧 Study suggests we need:
• Ethics integrated into core STEM curricula
• Peer-based learning (proven most impactful)
• Real-world challenges via internships/capstones

STEM education needs both technical rigor AND social responsibility
