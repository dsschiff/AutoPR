AI ethics education has exploded since 2018—but are we actually preparing students to navigate real-world ethical dilemmas? Our systematic review of 1,667 papers reveals a field full of promise but struggling with growing pains 🧵

[LINK]

---

📚 We analyzed content, pedagogy, and assessment practices across AI ethics programs. The results suggest significant gaps between educational goals and actual learning outcomes

[Attach image: img_0.jpg]

---

🔑 Programs focusing on broader societal issues—bias, fairness, privacy—show more impact than narrow "rules for algorithms" approaches. Higher-ed leads, but K-12 efforts are catching up

[Attach image: img_1.jpg]

---

🎓 Most effective teaching methods are hands-on: case studies, group projects, gaming & storytelling. These make abstract ethical principles tangible for students

---

❌ The assessment gap: While programs aim to develop ethical reasoning skills, few actually measure whether students are learning. Summative assessments dominate, formative feedback is rare

---

🚧 Key challenges include keeping pace with AI's rapid evolution, teaching abstract concepts to diverse audiences, educator shortages, and misalignment between teaching goals and assessment

[Attach image: img_2.jpg]

---

💡 What's needed: Better tools to measure behavioral impact, integration across all education levels, and policy support for formative assessments
