AI ethics education has exploded since 2018—but are we actually preparing students for real-world ethical dilemmas? Our systematic review of 1,667 papers reveals a field with promise but struggling with assessment gaps 🧵

[LINK]

---

📚 We analyzed content, pedagogy, and assessment across AI ethics programs. Results suggest significant misalignment between educational goals and learning measurement

[Attach image: img_0.jpg]

---

🔑 Programs focusing on broader societal issues—bias, fairness, privacy—show more impact than narrow technical approaches. Higher-ed leads comprehensive curricula

[Attach image: img_1.jpg]

---

🎓 Most effective methods are hands-on: case studies, group projects, storytelling. These make abstract ethical principles tangible for diverse learners

---

❌ The assessment gap: Programs aim to develop ethical reasoning, but few measure actual learning. Summative assessments dominate, formative feedback is rare

---

🚧 Key challenges: keeping pace with AI evolution, teaching abstract concepts, educator shortages, and misalignment between teaching goals and assessment practices

[Attach image: img_2.jpg]

---

💡 What's needed: Better behavioral impact measurement, cross-level integration, and policy support for formative assessments in AI ethics education
