AI ethics education has grown rapidly since 2018, but are we effectively preparing the next generation to navigate real-world ethical dilemmas? Our systematic review of 1,667 research papers reveals both promise and significant challenges. [LINK]

✅ Key findings from our analysis:

• Programs focusing on broader societal issues (bias, fairness, privacy) show greater impact than narrow technical approaches
• Most effective pedagogies are hands-on: case studies, group projects, and interactive storytelling
• Critical assessment gap: While programs aim to develop ethical reasoning skills, few measure whether students actually learn these competencies

➤ Major challenges include keeping pace with AI's rapid evolution, teaching abstract concepts to diverse audiences, and a shortage of trained educators.

➤ The field needs better tools to measure behavioral impact, integration across all education levels, and stronger alignment between teaching goals and assessment practices.

For policy practitioners and governance teams: This research suggests we need more formative assessment approaches and cross-disciplinary integration to build truly effective AI ethics education.
