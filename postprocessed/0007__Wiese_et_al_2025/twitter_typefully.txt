The AI job market is shifting beyond coding. Employers now demand skills in AI ethics and governance at unprecedented rates.

Our analysis of 4.4M+ job postings from 2018-2023 reveals what's driving this transformation 🧵

---

📊 The numbers tell a clear story:

• AI ethics roles: 35K (2018) → 109K (2022)
• AI governance roles: similar trajectory → 96K (2022)

Even as overall AI hiring dipped in 2023, these specialized roles remained stable.

[Attach image: img_1.jpg]

---

🏦 Finance and Information sectors lead the charge

Why? Highly regulated industries can't afford ethical lapses. They need professionals who understand both technical systems AND regulatory compliance.

---

⚖️ AI ethics ≠ AI governance (though they're complementary)

Ethics roles focus on:
• Data privacy
• Bias mitigation
• Fairness frameworks

Governance roles emphasize:
• Risk management
• Policy development
• Regulatory compliance

---

🎯 Three forces driving this demand:

• Governments rolling out AI regulations
• Companies recognizing public trust as critical for AI adoption
• Universities scrambling to create relevant training programs

💡 The opportunity: 100K+ professionals needed annually in these fields

Yet many organizations struggle to find qualified talent. For those looking to upskill, this represents a significant career opportunity where demand outpaces supply.
