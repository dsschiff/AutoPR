📈 The growth is striking:

• AI ethics roles: 35K (2018) → 109K (2022)
• AI governance roles: similar trajectory → 96K (2022)

Even as overall AI hiring declined in 2023, these specialized positions remained stable.

[Attach image: img_1.jpg]

---

🏛️ Key insight: AI ethics ≠ AI governance

Ethics roles emphasize fairness, privacy, bias mitigation
Governance roles focus on risk management, policy, compliance

Both require interdisciplinary thinking beyond pure technical skills.

---

🎯 Finance and Information sectors lead demand—regulated industries that can't afford ethical lapses drive this growth.

Three forces align: new regulations, corporate trust needs, and educational program development.

---

💭 The bigger question: Should every AI professional receive ethics/governance training, or keep these as specialized roles?

With 100K+ positions needed annually, this represents a significant opportunity for those looking to upskill.
