The AI job market is evolving beyond technical skills. Our analysis of 4.4M+ job postings from 2018-2023 shows employers increasingly demand expertise in AI ethics and governance. [LINK]

➤ AI ethics roles grew from 35K (2018) to 109K (2022)
➤ AI governance positions hit 96K in 2022
➤ Both remained stable even as overall AI hiring declined in 2023

✅ Key finding: These aren't interchangeable roles. Ethics positions emphasize data privacy, bias mitigation, and fairness frameworks. Governance roles focus on risk management, policy development, and regulatory compliance.

✅ Industry leaders: Finance and Information sectors dominate demand—highly regulated industries that can't afford ethical lapses drive this growth.

• Three forces align: Government regulations, corporate need for public trust, and educational institutions creating new programs
• The opportunity: 100K+ professionals needed annually, yet talent remains scarce

As AI adoption accelerates, ethics and governance expertise becomes non-negotiable for responsible development. Organizations seeking competitive advantage should invest in building these capabilities now.
