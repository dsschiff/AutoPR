Automation is reshaping the future of work—and Norbert Wiener saw it coming decades ago. [LINK]

🔧 The Challenge: Beyond Job Displacement
Automation isn't just about efficiency. It risks eroding meaningful work—the creativity, purpose, and social connections that make labor fulfilling. Wiener warned of a "factory without employees" where humans compete with their own creations.

💡 Wiener's Ethical Vision
In 1949, he wrote to union leader Walter Reuther: "I do not wish to contribute to selling labor down the river." His message was clear: technology isn't neutral. Its design must prioritize human well-being over profit.

📊 Key Insights from 2023 IEEE Workshop
• Automation benefits often concentrated, worsening inequality
• Regulation consistently lags behind innovation
• Persistent governance gaps in accountability
• Risk of reducing work to cold metrics rather than human values

🌟 The Opportunity
With the right frameworks, automation can expand access to education and healthcare, reduce drudgery, and address systemic inequalities. But this requires rethinking success metrics—moving from profit to human flourishing.
