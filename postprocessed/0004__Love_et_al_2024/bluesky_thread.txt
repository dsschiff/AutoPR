🔧 The problem isn't just job displacement. Automation risks eroding meaningful work—the creativity, purpose, and social connections that make labor fulfilling.

Wiener warned of a "factory without employees" where humans compete with their own creations.

---

💡 In 1949, Wiener wrote to union leader Walter Reuther: "I do not wish to contribute to selling labor down the river."

His message? Tech isn't neutral. Its design must prioritize human well-being over profit.

---

📊 Key insights from the 2023 IEEE Workshop on Wiener's legacy:

• Automation benefits often concentrated, worsening inequality
• Regulation lags behind innovation
• Governance gaps in accountability persist

---

⚖️ Think the Luddites were anti-technology? Wrong. Their fight wasn't against machines—it was against systems that used tech to exploit workers.

Same patterns of displacement and "progress for the few" persist today.

---

🔮 Wiener understood we can't fully predict automation's ripple effects. The takeaway? We need ethical foresight as an ongoing process, not a one-time act.

Stay adaptable, proactive, and humble.

---

🌟 Automation doesn't have to mean dystopia. With the right frameworks, it can expand access to education/healthcare, reduce drudgery, and address inequalities.

But this requires rethinking success metrics—from profit to human flourishing.
