New research across Germany, UK, and US reveals concerning gaps in AI literacy among university students 📊

Over 70% haven't taken an AI course, yet they'll enter AI-driven careers

Our findings suggest education isn't keeping pace

We surveyed 1,465 students on AI knowledge, confidence, attitudes, and experience

🇩🇪 Germany leads in actual AI literacy
🇺🇸 US shows highest confidence in AI skills
🇬🇧 UK demonstrates most skepticism toward AI

But confidence ≠ competence

---

The confidence gap is striking 🤔

US students often overestimate their AI abilities compared to actual knowledge

This overconfidence could lead to poor decisions in tech careers

Germany's approach balances competence with appropriate caution

---

AI literacy goes beyond coding 💡

It includes understanding ethics, limitations, and societal impacts

Yet ethics is the least-covered topic in AI courses

Only 6.6% of German students say ethics is addressed substantially

[Attach image: img_3.jpg]

---

Where do students learn about AI? 📚

Mostly informal sources and tools like ChatGPT

30% of German students never explore AI outside class

Only 3% of US students engage with AI daily

Interest exists, but structured education lags

---

Our study also validated a robust AI literacy assessment tool for global use

This could help institutions benchmark and improve AI education worldwide

But results show we need tailored approaches—not one-size-fits-all solutions ⚖️

---

Key recommendations:

✅ Bridge confidence-competence gaps with hands-on learning
✅ Emphasize AI ethics in curricula
✅ Fund comprehensive AI literacy programs
✅ Move beyond surface-level AI tool use

AI literacy is essential for future leaders
