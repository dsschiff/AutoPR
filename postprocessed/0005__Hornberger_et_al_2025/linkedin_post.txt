New multinational research reveals significant gaps in AI literacy among university students who will soon enter AI-driven careers. [LINK]

Our study of 1,465 students across Germany, the UK, and the US found striking differences:

🇩🇪 Germany leads in actual AI knowledge, likely due to better integration of AI coursework
🇺🇸 US students show highest confidence in AI skills
🇬🇧 UK students demonstrate the most skepticism toward AI

The concerning finding? Over 70% of students across all countries haven't taken an AI-related course, despite the technology's growing importance in virtually every field.

➤ Confidence doesn't equal competence: US students often overestimate their AI abilities compared to actual knowledge
➤ Ethics education lags: Only 6.6% of students say AI ethics is covered substantially in courses
➤ Learning happens informally: Students rely on tools like ChatGPT rather than structured education

For education leaders and policymakers: We need comprehensive AI literacy programs that go beyond tool use to include ethics, limitations, and societal impacts. The future workforce depends on it.
