AI education isn't one-size-fits-all 🎯

Our study of 1,465 students across US, UK & Germany reveals 3 distinct profiles when it comes to AI confidence.

Results suggest we need tailored approaches 👇

---

We find that AI self-efficacy depends on more than just knowledge 📚

Interest acts as the bridge connecting attitudes, literacy, and usage.

Simply using tools like ChatGPT boosts student interest ⬆️

---

Three student profiles emerged:

🚀 AI Advocates (48%): Tech-savvy, confident
⚠️ Cautious Critics (21%): Skeptical, low confidence
🤔 Pragmatic Observers (31%): Neutral, moderate interest

Each needs different educational approaches

[Attach image: img_1.jpg]

---

Demographics matter 📊

• Advocates: mostly male, STEM backgrounds
• Critics: overrepresented in humanities, predominantly female
• Access to AI education varies—Critics report least exposure

This has equity implications

---

For educators: AI programs need tailoring 🎯

• Advocates: encourage ethical thinking
• Critics: demystify AI, show relevance to non-tech fields  
• Observers: use hands-on experiences

Otherwise we risk leaving groups behind
