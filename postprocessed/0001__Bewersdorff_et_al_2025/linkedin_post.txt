AI education isn't one-size-fits-all. Our new study reveals why tailored approaches matter for building student confidence with AI.

[LINK]

✅ We surveyed 1,465 students across the US, UK, and Germany to understand how attitudes, interest, and knowledge shape AI self-efficacy

✅ Results suggest three distinct student profiles emerge:
• AI Advocates (48%): Tech-savvy and confident
• Cautious Critics (21%): Skeptical with low confidence
• Pragmatic Observers (31%): Neutral with moderate interest

✅ Key finding: Interest acts as the bridge connecting attitudes, literacy, and actual AI usage. Simply using tools like ChatGPT boosts student interest.

➤ Demographics matter: Advocates are mostly male STEM students, while Critics are overrepresented in humanities and predominantly female. Access to AI education varies widely.

➤ For educators and governance teams: Each profile needs different approaches. Critics need demystification and relevance to non-tech fields. Observers need hands-on experiences. Advocates need ethical frameworks.

If we don't address these different learning needs, we risk leaving entire groups behind in an AI-driven world. [Add coauthors] [Add funder]
