Our new analysis maps the chaos and proposes a roadmap for human-centered AI governance that actually works 🧵

---

The core problem: AI advances rapidly while governance lags behind in fragmented systems

• Redundant frameworks create confusion
• Ethical concepts get lost in translation
• Voluntary standards lack enforcement teeth
• Geopolitical divides block coordination

---

Challenge 1: Translating fairness, transparency, and well-being into actionable standards is genuinely hard

These values are contested and context-dependent. Standards risk oversimplifying complex ethics or enabling "ethics-washing" without addressing structural issues

---

Challenge 2: Voluntary compliance dominates

Without enforcement, organizations cherry-pick standards for reputational signaling rather than meaningful accountability. Superficial audits replace substantive change

---

Challenge 3: The "15 competing standards" problem creates decision paralysis

Organizations face a patchwork of frameworks with no clear guidance on prioritization or integration

[Attach image: img_1.jpg]

---

Challenge 4: The pacing problem is real

Standards take years to develop, AI risks emerge in months. Result? Obsolete frameworks that can't keep up with technological and societal shifts

---

Challenge 5: Geopolitical fragmentation blocks progress

EU, US, and China pursue divergent approaches. Cultural and institutional differences make global alignment nearly impossible

---

🚀 Our proposed solution: Human-centered, harmonized, adaptive governance

Step 1: Global collaboration among ISO, IEEE, OECD using crosswalk tools to map overlaps and reduce redundancy

[Attach image: img_2.jpg]

---

Step 2: Layered governance approach

High-level frameworks (NIST AI RMF) → domain-specific guidelines (IEEE 7010) → operational tools (model cards)

This ensures both scalability and adaptability across contexts

---

Step 3: Make governance agile

• Living documents that evolve with tech
• Rapid-response taskforces for real-time updates
• Regulatory sandboxes to test frameworks in controlled environments

---

Step 4: Strengthen auditing ecosystems

• Train independent accreditation bodies
• Promote transparency in audit results
• Use practical tools like datasheets to bridge principles and practice

[Attach image: img_3.jpg]

---

Step 5: Embed diverse stakeholder voices throughout

From marginalized communities to Global South perspectives—inclusive councils and citizen assemblies ensure AI works for everyone, not just tech elites

[Attach image: img_4.jpg]

---

What's at stake: Harmonized governance could transform AI into a trustworthy tool for societal good

But fragmented systems risk ethics-washing and widening inequities
