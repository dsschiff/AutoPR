Healthcare research faces a paradox: we're data-rich but insight-poor. Privacy laws and demographic gaps prevent researchers from fully utilizing available datasets. Synthetic data—AI-generated datasets that mimic real-world patterns—promises a solution. But our new analysis in The Hastings Center Report reveals significant ethical challenges.

[LINK]

➤ The Promise: Synthetic data offers privacy protection, models rare diseases, and fills demographic gaps without exposing real patient information.

➤ The Risks: Accuracy concerns for rare conditions, potential privacy leaks, bias amplification, and regulatory loopholes that could bypass key protections.

➤ The Stakes: Over-reliance on synthetic data might sideline real-world diversity, potentially deepening rather than solving equity issues.

Our analysis suggests synthetic data must complement, not replace, real-world datasets. This requires accuracy standards, robust privacy safeguards, transparent policies, and continued investment in diverse, representative data.
