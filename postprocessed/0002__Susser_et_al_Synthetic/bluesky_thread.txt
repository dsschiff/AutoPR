Healthcare research is drowning in data but can't use it due to privacy laws and demographic gaps. Synthetic data—AI-generated datasets—promises privacy protection and inclusivity. But our analysis reveals serious ethical risks 🧵

[LINK]

---

Synthetic data uses machine learning to create artificial datasets mimicking real patterns. Benefits include:

✅ Privacy protection
✅ Rare disease modeling
✅ Demographic gap filling
✅ AI training fuel

---

But the risks are significant:

• Accuracy issues for rare conditions
• Potential privacy leaks
• Bias amplification
• Regulatory loopholes from "non-identifiable" labeling

---

Over-reliance could sideline real-world data diversity, deepening rather than solving equity issues. Our Hastings Center Report analysis suggests synthetic data must complement, not replace, real datasets.

---

We need accuracy standards, privacy safeguards, transparent policies, and investment in diverse real-world data.
