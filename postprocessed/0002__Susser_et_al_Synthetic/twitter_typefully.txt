Healthcare research is drowning in data but can't fully use it due to privacy laws and demographic gaps. Enter synthetic data—AI-generated datasets that promise privacy protection and inclusivity. But is it worth the risks? 🧵

[LINK]

---

Synthetic data uses machine learning to create artificial datasets that mimic real-world patterns. The appeal is clear:

✅ Privacy protection
✅ Models rare diseases
✅ Fills demographic gaps
✅ Fuels AI innovation

---

But synthetic data comes with serious ethical concerns that we can't ignore 🚨

• Accuracy issues for rare conditions
• Potential privacy leaks
• Bias amplification from flawed source data
• Regulatory loopholes

---

The "non-identifiable" label could allow synthetic data to bypass key protections. And over-reliance might sideline real-world data diversity—deepening rather than solving equity issues.

---

The Hastings Center Report suggests synthetic data must complement, not replace, real-world data. We need:

📊 Accuracy standards
🔒 Privacy safeguards
📋 Transparent policies
💰 Investment in diverse datasets

---

Synthetic data could bridge healthcare gaps or deepen inequities. The choice is ours.

For AI governance researchers and policy practitioners: what safeguards do you think are most critical?
